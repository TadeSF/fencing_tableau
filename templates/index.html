FenceWithFriends helps you create and manage the fencing tournament of your dreams.

Easily organize and manage your TOURNAMENTS

FenceWithFriends makes it easy to manage big and small fencing tournaments with different rulesets, including (multiple) preliminary groups, repechage and placement matches. It tracks scores and calculates standings and results in real-time. Save time and focus on what matters most - the competition.

All Fencers get access to their very own Fencer-Hub, where they can find detailed information about the upcoming matches, statistics about their perfomance and most importantly the next piste they are fencing on.

It is free and open-source. There is no registration required. You just need a Startlist as a comma delimited CSV file. You can download a template and sample file and read the Instructions on how to format the Startlist CSV below.

How to get started

To get started, you just click on "New Tournament" above. For any questions regarding the process or the Web-Application, please refer to our Userguide. We are still in the writing process, but you even now can find detailed information about the App.

If you have any questions or suggestions, please contact us via E-Mail or on Github.

The Startlist File

The startlist file is a comma delimited CSV file. The first line contains the column names. If you don't want to spend hours in your text-editor counting and positioning commas, we recommend you our Startlist-Builder, that makes it very easy to create your Startlist-File on the go.

If you already have an EXCEL-Sheet or similar, you can also download the CSV-Template below. In case you just want to test how the App works, we also provided you a Startlist Example with imaginary fencers.

The Codebase

FenceWithFriends is a Python-based Web-Application hosted on a webserver in Germany. The Code is completely open source and available throug our documentation (where the code is explained) and Github (where we are collaborating and keeping our version history). We are very open to participation, so if you are a Programmer and interested in joining this fun project, reach out to us!
